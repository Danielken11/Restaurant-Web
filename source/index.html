OUR HISTORY
1919 In 1919, amidst Iceland's rugged beauty, Skadi's doors swung open, welcoming wanderers seeking warmth and sustenance. Nestled in the heart of Reykjavik, this humble restaurant bore witness to a century of change. Originally a haven for weary travelers, Skadi evolved with the times, preserving its legacy. Through wars and economic shifts, Skadi stood resilient, its walls echoing the laughter of locals and tales spun by generations. The menu, a testament to Icelandic culinary traditions, showcased the timeless flavors of fermented shark and hearty lamb stew. As the years marched on, Skadi became a living history book, adorned with artifacts and photographs capturing moments frozen in time.
Today, with its weathered facade and vintage charm, Skadi remains a cherished landmark. Its tables hold the memories of countless patrons, and its hearth continues to cast a warm glow, inviting both locals and visitors to share in the enduring story of Iceland's oldest culinary gem.
